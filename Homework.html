Pizza

Pizza (Italian: ['pittsa], Neapolitan: [pittsa]) is a savoury dish of Italian origin consisting of a usually round, flattened base of leavened wheat-based dough topped width tomatoes, cheese and often various other ingredients (such as anchovies, mushrooms, onions, olives, pineapple, meat, etc.), which is then baked at a high temperature, traditionally in a wood-fired oven.[1]A small pizza is sometimes called a pizzeta. A person who makes pizza is known as a pizzaiolo.
